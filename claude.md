We are building a live football debate platform called SetPiece. Two speakers argue a football topic head to head via voice, a live audience listens and votes on who made the better argument, and debaters earn an Elo rating based on their results.
The core loop is: a debate topic is posted, two speakers join and argue their side live via voice, the audience listens in real time, after the final round the audience votes on who won, the winner's rating goes up and the loser's goes down, and rankings update publicly.
Key features are live voice rooms with two speakers and unlimited listeners, timed debate rounds with opening statement, rebuttal and closing argument enforced by a visible countdown, audience voting after the debate with hidden tallies until close, an Elo rating system where your record is public and permanent, global and weekly leaderboards, and live audience chat.
The frontend stack is Next.js with Tailwind CSS. Use shadcn/ui only where it genuinely helps — forms, modals, dropdowns — not for everything.
The design system uses these exact colours. Background #060608, a slightly lighter surface #0c0c10, card background #0f0f14, border colour #1a1a22, primary accent green #00ff87, secondary accent red #ff2d55, primary text #ececec, and muted text #555566. The feel should be bold and premium like a sports product, not generic SaaS. Typography should be display-heavy using Oswald for headlines and Roboto Mono for labels and data. The overall atmosphere should feel like a stadium — high energy, high stakes, dark and electric.
